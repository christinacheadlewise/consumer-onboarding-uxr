# What Stops Customers Converting?

> Research question: What are the top reasons new consumers don't complete a transaction in the first 30 days?

## The short answer

For most people, nothing "stops" them — they just haven't needed Wise yet. They signed up in anticipation of a future event (trip, transfer) and are waiting for the trigger. A smaller group hit real friction. An even smaller group chose a competitor.

---

## Factor 1: They haven't needed it yet (largest group)

**~54% of drop-offs are waiting for a trigger event**

- 82% said "not yet, but I plan to"
- Most common open-end themes: "haven't travelled yet," "trip is in December," "waiting for the exchange rate"
- These aren't lost customers — they pre-registered and intend to convert

When we checked the onboarding flow data, 81% of these "not yet" respondents had actually already used Wise for something else. The survey question asked about their *specific first intent* — many found a different path to value.

**What this means for the team:** Re-engagement comms for this group should be triggered by travel signals or time-based nudges, not urgency. They're not stuck; they're waiting.

---

## Factor 2: The Invest product loses nearly half at onboarding

| Product | Drop-off rate |
|---|---|
| Card | 10% |
| Send | 9% |
| Receive | 8% |
| **Invest/Assets** | **47%** |

This is the only product with a structurally different drop-off rate. The gap is too large to be an onboarding flow issue — it points to a product-expectation mismatch. Customers arrive expecting something that Wise Assets doesn't deliver (or doesn't deliver quickly enough).

**What this means for the team:** Investigate what these customers expected vs what they found. This is likely a product/positioning question, not an onboarding UX one.

---

## Factor 3: Receive-money users find signup harder

When we look at signup ease ratings by task type, receive-money customers who dropped off rate the experience a full point lower on a 5-point scale:

| Task | Drop-off ease rating | Converted ease rating |
|---|---|---|
| Card | 4.39 | 4.47 |
| Send | 4.04 | 4.28 |
| **Receive** | **3.62** | **4.41** |

The verification flow may be harder for people whose first action is receiving — possibly because their use case doesn't fit the default onboarding assumptions.

**What this means for the team:** Look at the receive-first verification journey specifically. Are there extra steps or confusion points compared to the send/card path?

---

## Factor 4: Uncertainty about their use case

| "Was that one-time or regular?" | Drop-off | Converted |
|---|---|---|
| Regular | 49% | 63% |
| **Not sure yet** | **29%** | **17%** |

Drop-offs are 1.7x more likely to say "not sure" about how often they'll need Wise. They lack a clear, recurring reason to come back.

**⚠ Note on "regularly":** The team flagged that respondents may have interpreted this retrospectively ("I already do this regularly") rather than prospectively ("I plan to do this regularly"). The behavioural data supports this concern — see [Statistical relationships](correlations.md) for the full discussion.

---

## Factor 5: Information gaps

| "Did you need help?" | Drop-off | Converted |
|---|---|---|
| No, it was easy | 62% | 78% |
| Other (unspecified source) | 17% | 2% |

Drop-offs were less likely to find the process self-explanatory. The 17% citing "Other" help sources suggests information needs that aren't currently met by the product, help centre, or onboarding flow.

---

## Factor 6: Card delivery and physical access

From the open-ended responses:
- Cards lost in the mail
- Ordered too close to departure
- Using digital card but waiting for physical to arrive

This is a logistics issue, not a product issue. But it delays the moment of first use for card-seekers who travel.

---

## Factor 7: Verification friction

27% of customers who found signup difficult cited ID/verification/documents as the reason. Specific issues mentioned:
- Passport scanning failures
- Proof-of-address requirements
- Account pauses during document review
- BRP/visa flows not supported in-app

Notably, 100% of customers who hesitated specifically about verification *still adopted*. The friction is painful but not terminal — it depresses long-term engagement rather than blocking conversion.

---

## What about competitors?

Only 5 of 26 who truly didn't convert used another provider. The stated reasons:
- Service not available in country (1)
- No additional benefits (1)
- Other (3)

**Competitors are not the primary threat.** Non-conversion is about timing and product fit, not competitive loss.

---

## Revenue impact

| Group | Median lifetime revenue | n |
|---|---|---|
| Converted | £28.27 | 1,184 |
| Drop-off | £12.16 | 147 |

But 88% of "drop-offs" have generated some revenue. They eventually found a path — just not the one they originally intended.
